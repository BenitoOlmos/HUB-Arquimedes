========================================================================
GUIDE: ADDING YOUR PUMP.GLB MODEL
========================================================================

Place your downloaded centrifugal pump 3D model (.glb format) in this folder and name it:
  pump.glb

------------------------------------------------------------------------
HOW INTERACTION WORKS:
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Our React viewer uses raycasting (clicking) to query information about the pump parts.
It targets meshes in your GLB file based on their object/mesh names inside the 3D model:

1. 'volute_casing'   - The outer spiral housing of the pump.
2. 'impeller'        - The spinning blade element.
3. 'shaft'           - The long rotor rod.
4. 'mechanical_seal' - The seal assembly.
5. 'bearings'        - The rotor support bearings.
6. 'shaft_sleeve'    - The sleeve shielding the shaft.
7. 'wear_rings'      - The sealing wear rings.
8. 'suction_flange'  - The inlet flange connection.

To change which mesh maps to which DB entry, open:
  frontend/src/components/3D/ExplodedPump.jsx
Under the `useFrame` animation loop, adjust the matchers (e.g., `name.includes('impeller')`).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROCEDURAL FALLBACK:
------------------------------------------------------------------------
If no `pump.glb` file is found in this directory, the application will catch the error and automatically render a gorgeous, procedurally-drawn 3D model of a centrifugal pump. This ensures the app compiles and is fully interactive right away!
========================================================================
